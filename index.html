What this trip is all about

Türkiye straddles Europe and Asia — a country where Byzantine domes, Ottoman palaces, lunar valleys and a turquoise coastline all sit within a short flight of each other. Our plan threads three very different sides of it into one relaxed ~9-night loop, balancing buzzy, culture-packed days with proper downtime by the sea.

🕌

Istanbul

The only city that spans two continents. Soaring mosques, labyrinthine bazaars, ferry rides across the Bosphorus, and the country's best food and nightlife. It's where we both land, meet, and ease in.

🎈

Cappadocia

Surreal "fairy-chimney" valleys and cave hotels in central Anatolia. Famous for its dawn sky full of hot-air balloons, volcanic-soil vineyards, and 1,000-year-old rock-cut churches.

🏖️

The Turquoise Coast

Antalya & Fethiye on the Mediterranean — warm beaches, gulet boat trips through hidden coves, Roman ruins, and slow seaside evenings. Where we wind all the way down before flying home.

How we'll travel ✦

🔁 The routeIstanbul → Cappadocia → Coast → home. A loop that flows from culture, to wonder, to rest.
✈️ Getting aroundCheap 1–1.5h domestic flights link the three regions (Turkish Airlines, Pegasus, AJet).
🗓️ WhenEarly–mid September: warm and sunny, thinner crowds, balloon-friendly skies and lower fares.
⏱️ Pace~3–4 days per region, with mornings or afternoons left free so it never feels rushed.
💸 StyleMedium budget — boutique 4★ stays, a mix of street eats and nice dinners, a few splurge experiences.
💬 Good to knowLanguage is Turkish (English is widely understood in tourist areas); currency is the lira, and cards are accepted almost everywhere.
